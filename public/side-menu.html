Gameplay Introduction

Quick Start— Your goal is to reveal all safe tiles without detonating any mines. Click tiles to reveal them, and right-click to flag tiles you believe contain a mine.

Rules & Controls
  • Reveal: Left-click a tile to reveal what's underneath. If it's a mine you lose.
  • Flag: Right-click to mark a tile as a suspected mine; right-click again to remove the flag.
  • Numbers: Revealed numbers show the count of mines adjacent to that tile (including diagonals).
  • Auto-reveal (chording): If a revealed number has the same number of flags as its digit, clicking it will also reveal its unopened neighbors (this is called chording).
  • First click safety: In most variants, your first click is safe and opens at least one tile.
Cheat Mode

Press C to enable cheat mode, which reveals all mines on the board by flagging them.

How to Read Numbers

The number on a revealed tile tells you how many mines are in the 8 tiles around it. Use these numbers to logically deduce which nearby tiles are safe to open and which contain mines.

Basic Strategy
  • Start at corners and open spaces — corners have fewer adjacent tiles so they're easier to deduce.
  • Flag confidently — flags reduce uncertainty. If a 1 sits next to exactly one unopened square, that square is a mine.
  • Avoid guessing when possible — deduce using multiple numbers or patterns.
Common Patterns & Advanced Tips
  • 1-2-1 pattern:A row like 1-2-1 often means the two outer tiles are mines, and middle is safe; use this to remove ambiguity.
  • Edge reasoning:On the board edge, numbers apply to fewer tiles so the math is simpler.
  • 1.5-click:A common advanced technique is a fast chord — pressing both buttons (or double-click) to reveal surrounding tiles once you have flagged neighbors — use with care!
Teamplay Tips (for Multi-player)
  • Use signals and chat to coordinate: say when you flag or suspect a tile, so others can avoid accidental clicks.
  • When in doubt, share the reasoning: "This '3' is touching 3 unopened cells, two of which are already flagged — the third must be a mine."
Custom Mode Tips
  • Keep width/height manageable — our board scales with your viewport so large boards can still fit but may be tiny on small screens.
  • Keep "mines" <= width*height - 1 or the game can't start; we enforce that in the UI and server side.
  • When setting custom boards for tournaments, prefer known 'classic' dimensions for fairness.
